With the rise of the metaverse, Virtual Reality (VR), Augmented Reality (AR), and Mixed Reality (MR) have emerged rapidly, applied in navigation, entertainment, virtual events, video content, educational training, language translation, sensing, tracking, and more. These technologies have even advanced into the integration of VR, AR, and MR into Extended Reality (XR). As we actively strive to "break" the two-dimensional space of computer screens and attempt to integrate the two-dimensional virtual world into our three-dimensional reality, one may ask: Is it not the three-dimensional reality that is being absorbed into the two-dimensional world of computers, with human consciousness and emotional experiences gradually being influenced by this virtual two-dimensional world? This work centers around the visualization of brain consciousness space, connecting Arduino with UE5 to create a visual effect that models rational chemical molecular movements (biological neural transmission) and emotional, abstract imaginative spaces. It also attempts to use simple sensors as interactive devices to realize human stimuli in this brain consciousness space as a medium.
