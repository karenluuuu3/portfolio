痕跡 | Traces

The paper submissions have not been made public yet. 《痕跡》，從陶瓷開片工藝中汲取靈感，探索時間性與人為之間的美學對話。開片裂紋自然生成於陶瓷表面，其紋理如同時間的筆觸，描繪著自然與器物間的深層互動，承載豐厚的文化與歷史意涵。透過數位化形式，作品重新詮釋開片的時間性與審美特質，聚焦時間作用與人為介入所留下的痕跡，以數位展現物質性細膩且耐人尋味的美感。 《痕跡》將裂紋視為時間的具象化，模擬自然紋理隨時間累積的過程，並融入人為操作所賦予的獨特性，每一道裂紋如同風化的軌跡，記錄過往的記憶與變遷，喚起觀者對自然、時間及自身於時間長河中的痕跡。作品以裂紋生成的動態變化為核心，展現隨時序推進的視覺演化，深刻詮釋開片工藝的邏輯與美學。 作品進一步延伸裂紋的表現，創造多樣的視覺形式，包括水晶內雕、水波紋與皮膚經絡等，將裂紋從傳統陶瓷開片的框架中解放，置於多元載體之上，透過這些媒材的轉換，作品引導觀者重新關注裂紋的美感，並在不同情境中體驗時間與人為介入的獨特意涵，這不僅是對傳統工藝的當代詮釋，更結合數位媒材與程序化生成的特性，拓展裂紋作為時間與文化象徵的表現可能性，為其在當代數位藝術中的運用開啟了新的想像。 關鍵字：數位藝術、四元樹、陶瓷開片、多媒體 "Traces," drawing inspiration from the ceramic crazing technique to investigate the aesthetic interplay between temporality and human intervention. Crazing, with its naturally formed cracks on the surface of ceramics, serves as a visual manifestation of time, portraying the deep interaction between nature and objects, while carrying rich cultural and historical significance. Through digitalization, the work reinterprets the temporality and aesthetic qualities of crack patterns, focusing on the traces left by the passage of time and human intervention. The digital presentation highlights the material's delicate and thought-provoking beauty. This work views cracks as a tangible representation of time, simulating the accumulation of natural patterns over time while incorporating the uniqueness introduced by human intervention. Each crack serves as a weathered trace, recording past memories and transformations, prompting the viewer to reflect on nature, time, and their own traces in the passage of time. The focus of the work lies in the dynamic changes of crack formation, showcasing a visual evolution that progresses with the flow of time, offering a profound interpretation of the logic and aesthetics of the craquelure technique. The work further extends the expression of cracks by creating diverse visual forms, such as crystal engraving, ripples, and skin meridians. These cracks are liberated from the traditional ceramic framework and placed on various media, guiding the viewer to appreciate the beauty of cracks. Through these material transformations, the work allows an exploration of the unique significance of time and human intervention in different contexts. This not only serves as a contemporary interpretation of traditional craftsmanship but also combines digital media and procedural generation, expanding the expressive possibilities of cracks as symbols of time and culture, thereby opening up new imaginations for their use in contemporary digital art. Keywords: Digital art, Quadtree, Crazing, Aesthetic, Multimedia
