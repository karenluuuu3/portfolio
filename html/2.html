Tracing the Origin of the Sixth Fuel Factory along the Touqian River

The exhibition is currently being prepared and will be publicly available in June 2025. 📍作品名稱: 洄鄉 📍作品論述: 《洄鄉》以人與魚等物種的身影交織，牽動彼此，表現一種「是人非人，是魚亦非魚」的物種融合，彷彿一種無形的力量在所有生命中悄然流動，交錯成共生的網絡，意欲傳達洄游與回鄉的過程不僅是動植物的本能，也包含人類對源頭的回歸與尋找。 本作品以抽象輪廓勾勒物種型態，並於都市之景及水光之隙緩慢行走的行列表現物種「洄鄉」，影像如水波般延展，傳遞回溯源頭的力量。當觀眾站立於作品前，他們將成為這場討論的一部分，透過裝置偵測觀眾、於畫面中生成新角色，而後與物種行列一同前行，象徵人類歷史與自然的共融與延續。 📍Title of the Work: Echo-me 📍Work Statement: The work "Echo-me" interweaves the silhouettes of humans and fish, seamlessly merging them into a fusion of species that transcends the boundaries of human and fish. It conveys an invisible, yet powerful force that quietly flows through all life, intricately intertwining into a network of coexistence. The piece aims to express that the process of migration and return is not merely an instinctual behavior of animals and plants, but also reflects humanity's innate search for its origins and a yearning to reconnect with its roots. This work uses abstract contours to outline the forms of species, depicting their migration as they move slowly through urban landscapes and shimmering waters. The visuals have a rippling effect, conveying the force of tracing back to the source. When viewers stand before the work, they become part of this dialogue—the installation senses their presence, generating a new on-screen character that joins the procession, symbolizing the integration and continuity of human history and nature.
